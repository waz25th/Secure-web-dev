Gaius Julius Caesar

Born on 13/07/100BC, known as Julius Caesar; was a Roman politician, general and notable author of Latin prose, playing a critical role in the rise of the Roman Empire.

An alleged descendant of the goddess Venus, he became the head of his family at the age of 16.

The Gallic Wars

These were a series of campaigns waged by Julius Caesar in his two terms as proconsul of Cisalpine Gaul, Transalpine Gaul and Illyricum, against several Gallic tribes.

15th March, 44BC

Politicial Reforms

Holding both a dictatorship and tribunate and alternating between consulship and the preconsulship, he was appointed Dictator in 49 BC, resigned and then was appointed Dictator again in 48 BC.
