Sun Tzu

Sun Tzu, a name which in our current generation will remind you of his famous book: The Art of War, was a military strategist, a Chinese general and a philosopher who lived during the Spring and autumn period of ancient China.

His Legacy

Divided into 13 chapters, this book left behind by the war strategist has been applied to many military and intelligence agencies. With the most fascinating example of this being during the Sengoku period in Japan where daimyo Takeda Shingen became almost invincible mainly due to his study of the Art of War, which is listed on the Marine Corps Professional reading program, once known as the Commandant's Reading List and is a required reasing for all CIA officers.
