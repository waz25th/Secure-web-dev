Qin Shi Huang

Also known as the first Emperor of China, this man was responsible for the unification of China, with the name Qin Shi Huangdi meaning the first Emperor of China, this name came to him after he had conquered six other kingdoms within China.

The Great Wall of China

Due to the constant battle against the Xiongnu tribes in the north and north-west. In order to guard the Northern border, the construction of the enormous defensive wall was ordered. It was connected the the state walls that were previously built. This wall, became the precursor of the Great Wall of China and is estimated to have been the death of over a million people during its contruction.

His Legacy

During the reign of Qin Shi Huang, he ordered the most exisiting books to be burned, and decided to have 460 scholars killed, because of this Confucian scholars lable him a tyrant, making Huang an unliked figure for many centuries. However by the 19th centruy this had changed and it has been made clear how he was very important in China's step towards unification and standardization.

The Birth of the Qin dynasty

Born as Ying Zheng in 259 BC, Huang had become the Emperor of China at the young age of 13.

Huang's Tomb

Like many people of power within the Ancient era's of the world, during his life Huang had started work of a famous mausoleum which was to contain his burial chamber. It took 700,000 men and 38 years to construct. Surrounding his tomb are the 6,000 Terracotta warriors.
